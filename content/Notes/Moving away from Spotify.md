For various reasons, I'm trialling a turn away from Spotify. In its place, I will employ multiple pieces of software and ethos, I will own my music library and be a bigger financial contributor to artists.

To get my music, I will use SoulSeek. To play my music at home, I will use MusicBee. And to play music on my phone, I will use PowerAmp. I'm encountering a few problems and I need to write things down to work them out and I decided to do that writing here. 

## The Process
Generally, I will sit down at my computer and download a song I want to listen to. Once I've downloaded it, I'll move it to my Music folder, where I'll also organise it and fix any formatting issues. This part is good and fine. I can open MusicBee to listen to it and have an all-round great experience. Then I plug my phone in and sync my library. I can even sync my playlist files which reference the tracks in my library. 

## The Problem
Let's say I'm on the bus and I suddenly remember an album I used to listed to as a child. What do I do? The easy answer would be to load up my ad-free version of YouTube, listen to it, then add it to some sort of list so I can download it and sync it to my phone for the next time I want to listen to it. A method that's a bit more involved would be to use Seeker, a mobile SoulSeek client. I could then either use my mobile as a second p2p user or use my mobile to pull only from my main account (which may not always be online). I think I'll stick with the YouTube solution.

Another issue I foresee is Spotify Jams. While I can join a Spotify jam as a free user, I cannot create them. If I'm in a car, either they browse my library and add what they like or they add their phone and begin a jam.

And finally, quite often I'll scroll my playlists for something unique and go on to explore the album it's on. If I use a Spotify to SoulSeek tool, I won't have the album, just the individual track. That's a quality of life change, but still something I'd want long term.
