With much of our lives taken by the day-to-day, it's easy for us to become swept up in the happenings of life without stopping to assess where we place our trust. We trust Amazon with our purchases, we trust Google with our data, we trust Spotify with our music - I could go on. In an ideal world, these services are ran in alignment with citizen's interests, but what happens when that alignment is broken?

Take a look at this seemingly benign image below. From left to right, the six faces we see are Priscilla Chan (operational head of the Chan Zuckerberg Initiative and wife of Mark Zuckerberg), Mark Zuckerberg (Facebook & Meta CEO), Lauren Sánchez (Media personality and fiancée to Jeff Bezos), Jeff Bezos (Amazon CEO), Sundar Pichai (Google CEO), and finally, the world's wealthiest man, Elon Musk (Tesla, SpaceX, and X, formerly Twitter).

![[Untitled.jpg]]

Sure, these are very influential people, they must be at some party or fancy gathering. These billionaires are actually photographed here in the exclusive seats at Trumps 2025 Presidential Inauguration, usually reserved for family members and honoured guests. They're standing right now, of course, but this extremely public appearance is a clear approval of the populist figure currently pushing the USA into fascism. 

You're free to argue that point, but you'd have to provide me with some convincing arguments to change my mind about that. In short, I'm trying to say these are people I do not want to trust with my data, and I especially don't want to make these people more rich by giving them my money. Their services may be useful, but these people are rich enough, and I don't believe they are using their power or status for positive change. I'm unsure on Priscilla Chan - she seems to have some positive influence on Zuckerberg, convincing him to say he'll use a considerable portion of his wealth to support positive initiatives. That, however, was back in 2015, so I'm not sure how well that's going.

The money I give to Netflix, Spotify, Google, and Amazon specifically could easily be used to fund a home server where I can replicate their services in a more private, useful, and fun manner. I have recently began [[Moving away from Spotify|moving away from Spotify]] and I think [[I want to de-Google]]. The money I end up saving can then be given to smaller services and individual creatives whom I think deserve it far more.
