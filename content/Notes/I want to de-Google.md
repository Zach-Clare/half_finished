The grip of [the world's third-highest tech company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_largest_technology_companies_by_revenue) is too much for me to feel comfortable with. They have my emails, my photos - my phone's keyboard is connected to their servers for fucks sake. I would say I'm hopelessly connected, but I'm not *hopeless*.

[I write software](https://github.com/Zach-Clare) and I [[My custom Differential Evolution error function|write about it, too]]. Armed with a home server, I can replicate the very large majority of what Google does for me. If you're new to the subject of de-Googling, I think I would recommend PewDiePie's video titled [I'm Done with Google](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u_Lxkt50xOg). I know, PewDiePie? It's the first video of his I've seen in around 5 years and, given the topic, I'm happy I crossed paths with his content once again. Let's go over a few areas of Google's monopoly and outline where we can make the most impactful changes.
## Search
This is probably the most obvious service and also the easiest to replace. [StartPage](https://www.startpage.com/) allows you to use Google's search engine without the tracking, advertising, and AI summaries to get in the way. If you want to go a step further, you can use [DuckDuckGo](https://start.duckduckgo.com/), which uses their own indexing system and is thus de-coupled from Google entirely. [Ecosia](https://www.ecosia.org/) is another popular option based on Bing search with a green focus, but it's reliance on Microsoft is somewhat off-putting in this context. [SearXNG](https://github.com/searxng/searxng) is another tool, but I'm not very familiar with it.
## Photos
This is probably *my* biggest hurdle. Every photo I've ever taken on a mobile device is stored on Google Photos, so it feels like a big task to move them over to somewhere else. With that being said, tools like [Immich](https://immich.app/) offer a Free and Open-Source (FOSS) alternative, as long as you have a home service. I have a second PC very suited to being a home server - I just need storage. It would also be a good idea to have an remote encrypted backup in a separate location to guard against fire or theft. I have a number of trustworthy and tech-savvy friends that I think would be down for a mutual backup swap.
## Documents and Cloud Storage
There's an apparently very good alternative for Google Docs, Sheets, and Slides called [NextCloud](https://nextcloud.com/). They also offer a document storage area complete with sharing and access rights. It needs a home server but that's no huge barrier and will actually become fun once I have the time to do it. I could even host areas for my friends on here, though the platform really is best if it's your own.
## Email
[ProtonMail](https://proton.me/mail) feels like an easy alternative, but it's a simple shift of trust that might not sit right. In terms of self-hosting, [DoveCoat](https://dovecot.org/) seems to pop up online. I need to do some more reading about this, because I'd perhaps need my own domain name.

Also, I'd need some sort of redirect, right? And what about all the accounts where my email *is* my username? Would I need to reach out to each service and try to update it? I can't think of any specific services I'd need to change off the top of my head, but there's bound to be many. GitHub's will be fairly easy to change, I'm thinking about smaller services like [DBDesigner](https://www.dbdesigner.net/), for example.
## Android
Photos isn't my biggest hurdle, it's this. [GrapheneOS](https://grapheneos.org/) seems to be the most popular alternative, but it's a big change. This is probably something I'll do further down the line when I feel less attached.
